The 4C Framework

My proprietary stakeholder alignment system; developed over five years of delivery across Nigerian banking and fintech. Four steps that move every project from ambiguity to committed action.

C1 · Clarity

Surface the Real Problem

Most projects fail because the real problem is buried under its presenting symptom. My first engagement activity is a structured problem-definition session; peeling back assumptions until every stakeholder agrees on what we are actually solving, why it matters, and how we will measure success.

C2 · Conflict

Make Disagreement Productive

Unresolved conflict does not disappear; it migrates to your delivery phase and surfaces as scope creep. I create structured space for competing perspectives to surface during requirements workshops; where resolution is cheap and learning is high.

C3 · Consensus

Drive Shared Understanding

Through facilitated workshops and documented decision logs, I move stakeholders from divergent priorities to a shared, signed-off understanding of scope, priority, and acceptable trade-offs. No verbal agreements; only documented ones.

C4 · Commitment

Accountability, Not Just Agreement

Consensus without accountability is a meeting with no follow-up. I close every phase with RACI-mapped ownership, named sign-off owners, milestone checkpoints, and measurable acceptance criteria tied to business outcomes; not technical completions.
