Proprietary Methodology

The 4C Framework

Developed from five years of stakeholder alignment work across Nigerian banking; a repeatable system for moving projects from ambiguity to committed, accountable delivery.

C1 — Clarity
Surface the Real Problem
Most projects fail because the real problem is buried under its presenting symptom. My first engagement activity is always a structured problem-definition session with primary stakeholders; peeling back assumptions until we agree on what we are actually solving, why it matters, and how we will know we have solved it.
C2 — Conflict
Make Disagreement Productive
Unresolved conflict does not disappear; it migrates to your delivery phase and surfaces as scope creep or change requests. I create structured space for competing perspectives to surface during requirements workshops, where resolution is cheap and the learning is high.
C3 — Consensus
Drive Shared Understanding
Through facilitated workshops, requirement validation sessions, and documented decision logs, I move stakeholders from divergent priorities to a shared, signed-off understanding of scope, priority, and acceptable trade-offs. No verbal agreements; only documented ones.
C4 — Commitment
Accountability, Not Agreement
Consensus without accountability is a meeting with no follow-up. I close every engagement phase with RACI-mapped ownership, named sign-off owners, milestone checkpoints, and measurable acceptance criteria tied to business outcomes rather than technical completions.
